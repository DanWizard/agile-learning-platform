What is a Sprint

The heart of Scrum is a time-box called a Sprint. They can range anywhere from 1 to 4 weeks, and should stay consistent to allow teams to settle into a cadence for all of the different events that occur. A new Sprint starts immediately after the conclusion of the previous Sprint.

No changes are made that would affect the Sprint Goal. Development Team composition remains constant. Quality goals do not decrease as the days progress. Scope may be clarified / re-negotiated as more is learned. Sprint may be cancelled if the Sprint Gal becomes obsolete ( Product Owner). The scope of the teams efforts are protected by this time-box of effort. Timeboxing allows for focused efforts and making educated tradeoff
